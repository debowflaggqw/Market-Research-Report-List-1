<p><p>The coconut fibre market is categorized into several types, including brown fibre, white fibre, bristle coir, and others. Brown fibre, derived from mature coconuts, is coarse and often used in insulation, mats, and ropes. White fibre, sourced from younger coconuts, is finer and suitable for softer products like textiles and crafts. Bristle coir is stiffer, commonly used in brushes and brooms. Other types encompass specialty fibres used in gardening, upholstery, and eco-friendly products, reflecting diverse applications and increasing demand.</p></p>
