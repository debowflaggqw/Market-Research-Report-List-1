<p><p>Continuous Subcutaneous Insulin Infusion Devices (CSII) include normal pumps and patch pumps. Normal pumps are traditional devices that consist of a reservoir and a programmable delivery system, often attached to a belt or clipped to clothing, allowing for precise insulin delivery. Patch pumps, on the other hand, are more compact and adhesive, creating a discreet, tubeless option that adheres directly to the skin. Both types enable better glucose management by providing continuous insulin delivery tailored to the user's needs.</p></p>
