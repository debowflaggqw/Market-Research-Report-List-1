<p><p>Passenger Car Energy Recovery Systems encompass two primary market types: Automotive Regenerative Braking Systems and Automotive Waste Heat Recovery Systems. Regenerative braking systems capture kinetic energy during braking and convert it into electrical energy, improving vehicle efficiency and extending range. In contrast, waste heat recovery systems collect excess heat from the engine and exhaust, converting it into usable power, thereby enhancing overall energy efficiency. Both technologies contribute to reducing fuel consumption and lowering emissions in modern vehicles.</p></p>
