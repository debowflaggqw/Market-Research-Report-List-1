<p><p>Marine thermal imaging cameras are essential for various maritime applications, utilizing infrared technology to detect heat signatures. There are two main types: uncooled and cooled. Uncooled thermal cameras operate at ambient temperatures without the need for additional cooling mechanisms, making them compact and cost-effective. In contrast, cooled thermal cameras use cryogenic cooling to enhance sensitivity and image quality, catering to demanding environments. Both types have distinct advantages, offering versatility in navigation, surveillance, and environmental monitoring in marine settings.</p></p>
