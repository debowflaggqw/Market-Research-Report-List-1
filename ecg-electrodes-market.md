<p><p>The ECG electrodes market can be categorized by conducting medium and back-lining materials. Conducting mediums include liquid electrodes, which often have saline solutions for improved conductivity, and solid-gel electrodes, featuring a stable gel for consistent signal transmission. Back-lining materials affect comfort and adhesion; sponge back-lining uses soft, absorbent materials for patient ease, while non-woven back-lining, made from synthetic fibers, offers durability and breathability. Together, these factors influence the performance and usability of ECG electrodes in clinical settings.</p></p>
