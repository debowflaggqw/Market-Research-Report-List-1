<p><p>The calf prosthesis market comprises two main types: shell prostheses, also known as exoskeleton prostheses, and skeletal prostheses, referred to as endoskeletal or component prostheses. Shell prostheses are external structures that encase the residual limb, providing support and protection, often made from durable materials. In contrast, skeletal prostheses incorporate a lightweight internal framework, allowing for adjustability and customization. They typically consist of modular components that enhance functionality and comfort, catering to diverse user needs and activity levels.</p></p>
