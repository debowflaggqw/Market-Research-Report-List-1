<p><p>Centrifugal pumps and positive displacement pumps are two primary types of fluid movement devices used across various industries. Centrifugal pumps operate by converting rotational energy into kinetic energy to move fluids, making them ideal for high flow rates and low-viscosity liquids. They are commonly employed in applications involving water supply systems, chemical processes, and irrigation.</p><p>On the other hand, positive displacement pumps function by trapping a fixed amount of fluid and forcing it out, which makes them suitable for handling viscous fluids and providing consistent flow rates. They find use in industries such as oil and gas, food and beverage, and pharmaceuticals.</p><p>The Centrifugal Pump & Positive Displacement Pump Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 7.4% during the forecast period, driven by increasing demand in various sectors, advancements in pump technology, and an emphasis on energy efficiency. <p><p>Centrifugal and positive displacement pumps serve distinct market needs. Centrifugal pumps, including axial flow, mixed or radial flow, and peripheral pumps, use rotational energy to move fluids, ideal for high flow rates. Axial flow pumps move fluid along the pump axis, while mixed or radial pumps combine axial and radial flow principles. Peripheral pumps are suited for low-flow, high-head applications. Jet pumps utilize high-velocity fluid streams to lift liquids, often used in applications like well water extraction. Each type has specific advantages based on flow, pressure, and application requirements.</p></p>
