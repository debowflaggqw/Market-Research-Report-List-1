<p><p>The protein assays market encompasses various methods for quantifying protein concentrations. Dye-binding assays, like Bradford, use dye binding to protein for measurement. Copper-ion-based assays, such as Lowry's method, rely on the interaction between copper ions and protein to generate a color change, indicating concentration. Test strip-based assays offer a rapid, user-friendly option for field testing or point-of-care diagnostics. Other methods include BCA assays and UV absorbance techniques, catering to diverse research and clinical applications in protein analysis.</p></p>
