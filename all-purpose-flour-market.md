<p><p>The all-purpose flour market is divided into two main types: organic flour and ordinary flour. Organic flour is made from wheat grown without synthetic pesticides or fertilizers, appealing to health-conscious consumers seeking natural products. It often commands a premium price due to its production methods. Ordinary flour, on the other hand, is more widely available and cost-effective, made from conventionally cultivated wheat. It serves diverse culinary purposes but may contain additives, making it less desirable for some consumers focused on health and sustainability.</p></p>
